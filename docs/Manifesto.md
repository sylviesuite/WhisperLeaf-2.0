# WhisperLeaf Manifesto

AI should belong to the person using it.

For most people today, artificial intelligence lives somewhere far away — inside massive data centers operated by large companies.

Your questions.
Your documents.
Your ideas.

All sent to the cloud.

WhisperLeaf was created to explore a different path.

Your knowledge should stay with you.

Your personal notes, documents, research, and conversations form a private landscape of knowledge.

That landscape should not need to leave your computer to become useful.

WhisperLeaf runs locally so that your data remains where it belongs — with you.

Intelligence does not have to require surveillance.

Many modern AI tools depend on collecting enormous amounts of data.

WhisperLeaf takes a different approach.

It assumes that powerful tools can exist without tracking their users.

No hidden analytics.
No background data collection.
No behavioral profiling.

Technology should increase personal sovereignty.

When tools run locally, the user gains something important:

control.

Control over their data.
Control over their environment.
Control over how technology participates in their life.

WhisperLeaf is built around the belief that AI should empower individuals rather than centralize power elsewhere.

Calm tools are better tools.

The goal of WhisperLeaf is not to overwhelm the user with complexity.

It should feel quiet, minimal, and reliable.

A place where you can think clearly, explore ideas, and work with your knowledge without distraction.

The future of AI may be personal.

Instead of a handful of enormous systems serving everyone, the future may include millions of personal AI environments.

Small.
Local.
Private.

WhisperLeaf is a small step toward that possibility.

---
